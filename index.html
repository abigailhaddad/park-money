National Park Service
Spent $276M in DC
This Fiscal Year

USAspending.gov shows NPS awarded 140 contracts in Washington D.C. in FY2026 — fountains, fireworks, and renovations — as the administration redirected park money to the capital. Internal budget documents obtained by The Atlantic show DC spending up 92% while other parks fell 68%.

The Claim

Trump said he'd pay for it himself.

"Paid for by me." — President Trump, when asked about the White House walkway replacement, March 2026
The Reality

Budget documents obtained by The Atlantic reveal taxpayers paid $689,232 for the walkway alone — part of a $1.3 million project covering stone and masonry repairs and new door hardware. A year earlier, taxpayers spent $347,503 to remove stucco from the colonnade wall, clearing the way for Trump to affix gold frames and plaques mocking his predecessors. Public contracting records corroborate both the scale and character of the spending.

October – November 2025

FY2026 started quietly in D.C.

$222K

The fiscal year opened with just $222,384 in new NPS contracts for Washington D.C. — routine fountain component replacements on the National Mall.

December 2025

Then $59 million hit in one month.

$59.2M

A single contractor, Terra Site Constructors LLC, received seven fountain rehabilitation contracts totaling $55.2M between December 5 and 11. Three contracts were awarded on the same day: December 11th.

March – April 2026

A second wave followed in spring.

$37M

March brought $16.7M more — Logan Circle rehabilitation, turf maintenance, sinkhole repairs. April added $20.3M including a $14.7M contract to Atlantic Industrial Coatings to restore the Lincoln Reflecting Pool.

Terra Site Constructors LLC received $59.4M in NPS contracts in Washington D.C. across FY2026 — nearly all of it in a seven-day window in December 2025. Seven separate fountain rehabilitation contracts were issued under project NAMA 350892 (National Mall fountain program).

About This Data

Methodology

What this site measures: Contracts awarded by the National Park Service with a Washington D.C. place of performance, pulled from the public USAspending.gov API. This is not the same as total NPS project spending in D.C. — which would also include grants, intra-agency transfers, and revolving fund expenditures not captured in public contract records.

Contract data: Retrieved from api.usaspending.gov using the /api/v2/search/spending_by_award/ endpoint. Filters: awarding sub-tier agency = "National Park Service"; award types A, B, C, D (contracts); place of performance = Washington, D.C.; FY2026 (Oct 1 2025 – Jun 2026). Data retrieved June 2026.

Background reporting: The 92%/68% spending shift and the 900+ unfunded projects come from Michael Scherer's June 2026 investigation in The Atlantic , based on internal NPS budget documents. Those figures reflect total project spending from NPS's internal systems, not just public contract data.

Budget data: NPS appropriation data reviewed via BlazingStar's public SF-132 apportionment mirror. The FY2026 "Operation of the National Park System" account (TAFS 014-2026-2026-1036) shows $2.6B in total budgetary resources as of April 2026, per P.L. 119-74.

Contract amounts reflect total obligated value at time of query. Multi-year contracts (e.g., Consigli's Lincoln Memorial rehabilitation, started Dec 2022) appear in FY2026 results when they had activity in the period; their amounts include prior-year obligations. The timeline and vendor sections focus on contracts with a start date in FY2026 (Oct 1, 2025 or later).
