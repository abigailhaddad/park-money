This Fiscal Year

USAspending.gov shows NPS awarded 140 contracts in Washington D.C. in FY2026 — fountains, fireworks, and renovations — as the administration redirected park money to the capital. Internal budget documents obtained by The Atlantic show DC spending up 92% while other parks fell 68%.

The Claim

Trump said he'd pay for it himself.

"Paid for by me." — President Trump, when asked about the White House walkway replacement, March 2026
The Reality

Budget documents obtained by The Atlantic reveal taxpayers paid $689,232 for the walkway alone — part of a $1.3 million project covering stone and masonry repairs and new door hardware. A year earlier, taxpayers spent $347,503 to remove stucco from the colonnade wall, clearing the way for Trump to affix gold frames and plaques mocking his predecessors. Public contracting records corroborate both the scale and character of the spending.

Procurement Details

A closer look at who competed — and who didn't.

USAspending records include each contract's competition status and justification code. For roughly 30% of the dollars awarded this fiscal year in DC, NPS either skipped competitive bidding entirely or cited a statutory exemption to avoid it.

No-bid emergency · FAR 6.302-2

The Reflecting Pool was declared an emergency.

$15.9M awarded without competitive bidding

In April 2026, NPS awarded a $14.2 million contract to Atlantic Industrial Coatings LLC to restore the Lincoln Reflecting Pool — without soliciting competing bids. The official justification: "unusual and compelling urgency," a procurement exception reserved for genuine emergencies where delay would cause serious harm. A second no-bid contract for the same pool, $1.7 million for nano-coating work, went to a different vendor (Green Water Solutions) under the same urgency justification one week later.

The urgency exception exists so the government can move fast in a genuine crisis. Critics of the provision note it can also be invoked to steer work to a preferred vendor.

1 bidder per contract · "Full and open after exclusion of sources"

Terra Site competed — but no one else showed up.

7 fountain contracts · 1 offer each

NPS classified each of Terra Site's seven December fountain contracts as "full and open competition after exclusion of sources." That designation means certain vendors were excluded from the pool before solicitation — typically non-small businesses under a set-aside program. Despite the competition label, FPDS records show each contract received exactly one offer. One company applied for each of seven contracts worth a combined $55 million.

There is nothing automatically improper about a one-bid award. But the pattern — seven separate procurements, same contractor, same week, all single-bid — is notable.

Executive Order 14398

Trump's beautification order shows up across the contract record.

$5.4M in contracts citing EO 14398

Executive Order 14398, which Trump signed directing federal agencies to beautify public spaces and fly flags at official buildings, appears explicitly in the description of at least five NPS DC contracts: $591k for flagpole replacements at Fort Washington and the Mall, $1.2M for expanded security guard services, $90k for fountain maintenance, $72k for anti-scale fencing installation, and a $3.5M modification to a Siemens energy contract. Several were added as modifications to existing agreements rather than as new competitive solicitations.

About This Data

Methodology

What this site measures: Contracts awarded by the National Park Service with a Washington D.C. place of performance, pulled from the public USAspending.gov API. This is not the same as total NPS project spending in D.C. — which would also include grants, intra-agency transfers, and revolving fund expenditures not captured in public contract records.

Contract data: Retrieved from api.usaspending.gov using the /api/v2/search/spending_by_award/ endpoint. Filters: awarding sub-tier agency = "National Park Service"; award types A, B, C, D (contracts); place of performance = Washington, D.C.; FY2026 (Oct 1 2025 – Jun 2026). Data retrieved June 2026.

Background reporting: The 92%/68% spending shift and the 900+ unfunded projects come from Michael Scherer's June 2026 investigation in The Atlantic , based on internal NPS budget documents. Those figures reflect total project spending from NPS's internal systems, not just public contract data.

Budget data: NPS appropriation data reviewed via BlazingStar's public SF-132 apportionment mirror. The FY2026 "Operation of the National Park System" account (TAFS 014-2026-2026-1036) shows $2.6B in total budgetary resources as of April 2026, per P.L. 119-74.

Contract amounts reflect total obligated value at time of query. Multi-year contracts (e.g., Consigli's Lincoln Memorial rehabilitation, started Dec 2022) appear in FY2026 results when they had activity in the period; their amounts include prior-year obligations. The timeline and vendor sections focus on contracts with a start date in FY2026 (Oct 1, 2025 or later).
